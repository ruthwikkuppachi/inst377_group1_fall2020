What is the Carbon Lockdown Project?


Answering that requires a bit of context. At our current rate of greenhouse gas emissions, our planet is headed for catastrophe. Even some of the best case scenarios for realistic emission reductions can look unpleasant, with difficult choices and unforeseen consequences at every turn. Improving on this grim outlook will require the use of every advancement and technology in humanityâ€™s arsenal. One of the best weapons is a group of innovations called Negative Carbon Technologies (NETs). The basic concept is to take Carbon Dioxide, either from the atmosphere or at the production source, capture it, and put it into long term storage. A Carbon Lockdown if you will.


This is where the Carbon Lockdown Project (CLP) comes in. The goal of the CLP is to encourage the development and use of NETs. Educate the public on their current status and future breakthroughs. Facilitate conversations between policymakers, business leaders, and scientists in the field. Push for progress and achieve a Carbon Lockdown.
